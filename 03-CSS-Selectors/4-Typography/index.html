In the late summer of the year we lived in a house in a village that look accross the river & the plan to the mountains. in the bed of the river there war pebbles and boulders, dry and white in the sun, and the water was clean and swiftly moving and blue in the channels. Troops went by the house and down the road and the dust they raised powdered the leavs of the trees. The trunks of the tree too were dusty and leaves fell early that year and we saw the troops marching along the road of the dust rising and leaves, stirred by the breeze, falling and the solders marching and afterward the road bare and white except for the leaves.

The plan was rich with crops; there were many orchards of fruits trees and beyound the plain mountains were down and bare. There was fighting in the mountains and at night we could see the fleashes from the artiellery. In the dark it was like summer lightning, but the nights were cool and there was not the feeling of a storm comming.
